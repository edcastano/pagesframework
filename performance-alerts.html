Performance Alerts

Understand warning signs and get recommendations to improve page performance

📊 Note: These metrics and benchmarks are educational guidelines. Actual performance tracking requires access to LinkedIn Page Analytics. Use these guidelines to interpret your real data.

🚨 Critical Alerts

Address these issues immediately as they significantly harm your page effectiveness

Critical

Dormant Page (No Posts in 90+ Days)

Your page hasn't posted in over 3 months. This signals to LinkedIn and your audience that the page is abandoned.

Why This Matters:

  • Algorithm deprioritizes inactive pages in search and recommendations
  • Followers stop engaging and may unfollow
  • New visitors question if your business is still active
  • Wastes your existing follower base

Immediate Actions:

HIGH: Decide: reactivate with content plan, merge with Company Page, or sunset gracefully with final post explaining transition
HIGH: If reactivating: create and schedule 10-15 posts immediately to rebuild momentum
HIGH: Assign a dedicated owner responsible for maintaining posting cadence
Critical

Declining Follower Count

Your page is losing more followers than it's gaining, indicating content isn't resonating or page is becoming irrelevant.

Common Causes:

  • Content doesn't align with follower expectations
  • Posting too frequently (spam perception)
  • Overly promotional content with no value
  • Page pivot without communicating to audience

Immediate Actions:

HIGH: Audit last 20 posts - identify which had lowest engagement and why
HIGH: Survey followers (if list available) to understand their interests
MEDIUM: Reduce posting frequency if > 5x/week and test quality over quantity
MEDIUM: Shift content mix: increase thought leadership and educational content, decrease promotions
Critical

Engagement Rate Below 1%

Your posts are getting less than 1% engagement (likes + comments + shares divided by impressions), well below industry benchmarks.

HIGH: Add clear calls-to-action: ask questions, request opinions, invite comments

MEDIUM: Respond to every comment within 2 hours to boost algorithmic ranking

⚠️ Warning Alerts

Address these issues soon to prevent performance degradation

Warning

Inconsistent Posting Schedule

Your posting frequency varies wildly (e.g., 5 posts one week, then 0 for two weeks). Consistency matters more than volume.

Actions to Take:

MEDIUM: Set a realistic posting schedule you can maintain (1-2x/week is better than bursts)
MEDIUM: Use LinkedIn's scheduling feature to maintain consistency during busy periods
LOW: Batch-create content: make 8-10 posts in one session to build a buffer
Warning

Low Reach Compared to Follower Count

Your posts only reach 10-20% of your followers, indicating algorithm isn't prioritizing your content.

Why This Happens:

  • LinkedIn algorithm deprioritizes pages with low historical engagement
  • Content isn't generating engagement signals (likes, comments, shares) quickly
  • Posting at suboptimal times when followers aren't active

Actions to Take:

MEDIUM: Encourage employees to engage with posts within first hour (this signals quality to algorithm)
MEDIUM: Tag relevant employees or partners in posts (when appropriate) to expand reach
LOW: Experiment with posting times - analyze when your audience is most active
Warning

Low Click-Through Rate on Posts

Posts with links get impressions but very few clicks, suggesting content isn't compelling enough or CTA is weak.

Actions to Take:

MEDIUM: Tease value in post copy before link - explain what they'll learn/gain
MEDIUM: Test native content vs. links - LinkedIn algorithm favors keeping users on platform
LOW: Use clear CTAs: "Read our guide on..." rather than "Check this out"

Performance Recovery Action Plan

If multiple alerts are triggered, use this 30-day recovery plan

Week 1: Assessment & Quick Wins

  • Audit last 30 posts - identify top 5 and bottom 5 by engagement
  • Create 10 posts based on successful content patterns
  • Schedule posts for consistent timing (same days/times weekly)
  • Invite team members to engage with posts within first hour

Week 2-3: Content Optimization

  • Test 3 different content formats (video, carousel, long-form text)
  • Increase thought leadership content to 40% of posts
  • Add clear CTAs to every post (question, poll, opinion request)
  • Respond to all comments within 2 hours

Week 4: Analysis & Adjustment

  • Review metrics: follower growth, engagement rate, reach
  • Double down on what worked, eliminate what didn't
  • Create content calendar for next 30 days
  • Set up monthly performance review cadence

✓ Remember:

Performance recovery takes time. Focus on consistent improvement over 2-3 months rather than expecting overnight results. Quality content, posted consistently, with active community management will yield results.
