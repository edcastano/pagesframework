Real-World Examples

Learn from successful organizations and how they structure their LinkedIn presence

Microsoft

Enterprise, Product-Led

Page Architecture:

  • Company Page: Microsoft (employer brand, corporate news, culture)
  • Showcase Pages: Microsoft Azure (cloud division), Microsoft 365 (productivity suite), Microsoft Gaming (Xbox ecosystem)
  • Product Pages: Individual products like Microsoft Teams, Power BI, Visual Studio where each has dedicated PMM and active roadmap

Rationale:

  • Showcase Pages differentiate major business units with distinct brand identities
  • Product Pages target specific buyer personas (e.g., IT admins for Teams, data analysts for Power BI)
  • Governance team approves all new Pages; each has dedicated owner with budget

Deloitte

Enterprise, Service-Led

Page Architecture:

  • Company Page: Deloitte (employer brand, thought leadership, firm-wide initiatives)
  • Showcase Pages: Deloitte Consulting, Deloitte Risk & Financial Advisory, Deloitte Tax (major practices)
  • Service Pages: Deloitte Digital (digital transformation services), Deloitte Analytics (data consulting)

Rationale:

  • Showcase Pages represent major practice areas with independent P&Ls
  • Service Pages highlight specialized offerings requiring credibility-building content
  • Each Page owned by practice leader responsible for engagement strategy

Figma

SMB/Mid-Market, Product-Led

Page Architecture:

  • Company Page only: Figma (combines employer brand, product updates, design community content)

Rationale:

  • Single flagship product with unified brand identity
  • Target audience (designers, product teams) highly cohesive
  • Creating separate Product Page would fragment audience without adding value

Accenture

Enterprise, Hybrid (Services + Products)

Page Architecture:

  • Company Page: Accenture (employer brand, corporate vision, talent acquisition)
  • Showcase Pages: Accenture Strategy (consulting practice), Accenture Interactive (digital agency), Accenture Operations (BPO)
  • Service Pages: Specialized offerings like cloud migration, cybersecurity consulting
  • Product Pages: Proprietary platforms like myConcerto (digital business platform)

Rationale:

  • Showcase Pages for major business units operating as distinct brands
  • Service Pages emphasize advisory credibility
  • Product Pages for scalable software offerings with feature-driven content

BrightPath Marketing

SMB, Service-Led

Page Architecture:

  • Company Page: BrightPath Marketing (employer brand, case studies, agency culture)
  • Service Page: BrightPath SEO Services (separate Page because it targets technical SEO buyers vs general marketing directors)

Rationale:

  • SEO service targets distinct persona (technical marketers) with specialized content needs
  • Agency has dedicated SEO practice lead who can sustain unique thought leadership
  • Other services (social media, content marketing) featured on Company Page

Sarah Chen Leadership Coaching

Solopreneur, Service-Led

Page Architecture:

  • Company Page only: Sarah Chen Leadership Coaching (personal brand, client testimonials, thought leadership)

Rationale:

  • Single service offering closely tied to founder's personal brand
  • Cannot sustain content velocity across multiple Pages
  • Target audience (executives seeking coaching) follows for Sarah's expertise, not a separate service brand
