Audience Targeting Tutorial

Learn how to reach specific audience segments without creating multiple pages

What is Audience Targeting?

Audience Targeting allows you to tailor your LinkedIn posts to specific segments of your followers based on demographics, location, industry, and more. This is an excellent alternative to creating multiple Showcase Pages when you can't maintain the required posting frequency.

Step-by-Step Guide

1

Access Your Company Page Admin Dashboard

Navigate to your LinkedIn Company Page and click on "Admin tools" in the top navigation bar. This is where you'll create and manage all your posts.

üìä Visual: Screenshot of Company Page with Admin Tools highlighted

2

Start Creating a New Post

Click "Create a post" or the compose icon. Write your post content as you normally would, including any images, videos, or links.

3

Enable Targeted Audience Settings

Look for the targeting icon (usually represented by a target or filter symbol) in the post composer. Click it to open the targeting options.

üéØ Visual: Post composer with targeting icon highlighted

4

Select Your Targeting Criteria

Choose from multiple targeting options to reach your desired audience:

üìç Geography

Target up to 150 specific locations, from countries down to cities. Perfect for regional campaigns or office-specific announcements.

üè¢ Company Size

Reach companies based on employee count (e.g., 1-10, 11-50, 51-200, etc.). Great for tailoring messages to SMBs vs. enterprises.

üëî Seniority Level

Target by job seniority: Entry, Senior, Manager, Director, VP, C-level, or Owner. Ideal for executive-focused content vs. practitioner-level content.

üåê Language

Reach followers who use LinkedIn in specific languages. Essential for multilingual organizations.

üè≠ Industry

Target specific industries like Healthcare, Technology, Finance, Manufacturing, etc. Perfect for industry-specific solutions.

5

Review and Publish

Review your targeting selections to ensure they match your intended audience. LinkedIn will show you an estimated reach. When ready, click "Post" to publish to your targeted audience.

Use Cases for Audience Targeting

üåé Regional Office Announcements

Instead of creating separate Showcase Pages for each regional office, use geographic targeting to share office-specific news, events, or job openings with followers in that region.

üéØ Product-Market Fit Content

If your product serves different company sizes differently, create targeted posts highlighting SMB use cases for smaller companies and enterprise features for larger organizations.

üíº Seniority-Based Messaging

Share thought leadership content targeted at C-level executives, while sending technical deep-dives to individual contributors and managers.

üí° Pro Tip: Audience Targeting is most effective when you have at least 300+ followers. If you're just starting out, focus on building your follower base first, then implement targeting strategies.

When to Use Targeting vs. Creating Separate Pages

Use Audience Targeting When... Create Separate Page When...
You can't post 2-3x per week consistently You can maintain 2-3 unique posts per week
Audiences have significant overlap Audiences are completely distinct
The offering shares the same brand identity The entity has its own brand identity
Limited resources for page management Dedicated owner and budget allocated
